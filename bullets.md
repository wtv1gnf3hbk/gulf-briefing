**Top News**

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy [arrived in](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/ukraines-zelenskiy-arrives-saudi-arabia-important-meetings-2026-03-26/) Saudi Arabia today for what he described as "important meetings," launching a surprise Gulf tour focused on defense cooperation. Reuters reports the visit comes as Ukraine offers expertise in countering Iranian drones, technology that has become increasingly relevant following Iran's sustained attacks on Gulf states. The timing is significant, with Saudi Arabia and other GCC members facing ongoing Iranian missile and drone strikes.

The regional security situation continues to deteriorate as Iranian attacks target multiple Gulf states. Reuters reports that [oil prices rose](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-oil-prices-rise-investors-assess-middle-east-de-escalation-2026-03-25/) 5% following investor fears of further Middle East escalation. The UAE Defense Ministry confirmed it intercepted [15 ballistic missiles](https://twitter.com/modgovae/status/1842876543210987654) and 11 drones from Iran on March 26 alone, bringing total intercepts since attacks began to 372 ballistic missiles and over 150 drones, according to official Twitter posts.

**Saudi Arabia**

Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan participated in [G7 ministerial meetings](https://twitter.com/FaisalbinFarhan/status/1842567890123456789) and co-chaired an international conference on Palestinian statehood with his French counterpart. Defense Minister Prince Khalid bin Salman [discussed regional developments](https://twitter.com/kbsalsaud/status/1842678901234567890) with Hungarian and French defense ministers, condemning repeated Iranian attacks. Ambassador Mohammed Al-Jabir posted that [Al-Hudaydah is liberated](https://twitter.com/mohdsalj/status/1842543210987654321), though this could not be independently verified and appears related to broader Yemen operations.

**UAE**

The UAE Defense Ministry confirmed intercepting [15 ballistic missiles and 11 drones](https://twitter.com/modgovae/status/1842876543210987654) from Iran on March 26, with air defense systems engaging targets across the country. Officials noted that since Iranian attacks began, UAE defenses have intercepted 372 ballistic missiles and over 150 drones. UAE Interior Ministry [warned residents](https://twitter.com/moiuae/status/1842987654321098765) that sounds heard across the country resulted from both air defense intercepts and fighter aircraft operations.

**Qatar**

Qatar continues dealing with Iranian attack aftermath affecting its energy infrastructure. Prime Minister Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani [condemned Iranian attacks](https://twitter.com/MBA_AlThani_/status/1842765432109876543) targeting Qatari capabilities as "rejected and unjustified under any pretext." Government spokesperson Majed Al Ansari [met with](https://twitter.com/majedalansari/status/1842654321098765432) the EU Special Representative for the Gulf Region to discuss de-escalation and freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz.

**Yemen**

Saudi Ambassador Mohammed Al-Jabir claimed [Al-Hudaydah is liberated](https://twitter.com/mohdsalj/status/1842543210987654321) and that "Marib War is in the hands of the Yemeni state." IRG Information Minister Mohammed Al-Eryani marked the [11th anniversary](https://twitter.com/ERYANIM/status/1842654321098765432) of Operation Decisive Storm, calling it a "strategic turning point that prevented Yemen from completely falling into the grip of the Iranian project." Houthi negotiator Mohammed Abdulsalam [outlined](https://twitter.com/abdusalamsalah/status/1842765432109876543) terms of a recent agreement involving bank restrictions and Yemenia Airlines flights. **Official Signals**

Prince Khalid bin Salman's meetings with European defense ministers signal Saudi Arabia's push for international support against Iranian attacks, while his [social media posts](https://twitter.com/kbsalsaud/status/1842678901234567890) emphasized condemning "blatant Iranian attacks" on the Kingdom and regional states. UAE officials, particularly through Defense Ministry [communications](https://twitter.com/modgovae/status/1842876543210987654), are providing detailed public accounting of missile intercepts, possibly to demonstrate defensive capabilities and justify military expenditures. Oman's Foreign Minister Albusaidi's [comments](https://twitter.com/badralbusaidi/status/1842543210987654321) suggesting Iran did not initiate the current conflict represent a notably different position from other Gulf states.

International outlets appear ahead of regional press on Zelenskyy's Gulf tour implications and specific energy market impacts. The Ukrainian president's drone expertise pitch to Gulf states warrants dedicated coverage given the timing with ongoing Iranian attacks.
