**Gulf Bureau Daily Briefing - March 26, 2026**

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy [arrived in](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/ukraines-zelenskiy-arrives-saudi-arabia-important-meetings-2026-03-26/) Saudi Arabia for what Reuters reports are "important meetings," as Ukraine seeks to leverage its anti-drone expertise gained from defending against Iranian-supplied weapons. The [surprise visit](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/zelenskiy-kicks-off-surprise-gulf-states-tour-with-defense-pitch) marks the start of a Gulf tour focused on defense cooperation, according to Bloomberg, as Ukraine positions itself as a partner against Iranian military technology that has been used both in the Russia-Ukraine conflict and regional attacks.

Meanwhile, oil prices [surged 5%](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-oil-prices-rise-investors-assess-middle-east-de-escalation-2026-03-25/) as investors fear further Middle East escalation, with Reuters reporting heightened concerns about regional stability. Iran continues to assert control over maritime routes, with Trump claiming Iran is "begging" for a deal while Tehran [issues new](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/26/as-trump-claims-us-winning-tehran-asserts-legal-right-over-hormuz-strait) demands regarding Strait of Hormuz sovereignty, according to Al Jazeera.

**Energy & Economy**

• Saudi Arabia's oil sales to major Asian buyers [will drop](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/saudi-oil-sales-to-top-asia-buyers-to-drop-on-hormuz-disruptions) due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions, Bloomberg reports, as regional tensions affect energy supply chains.

• Iran war damage to Qatar [hits global](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/iran-war-damage-qatar-hits-global-lng-outlook-upends-asia-demand-growth-2026-03-26/) LNG outlook and upends Asian demand growth, according to Reuters, significantly impacting global energy markets.

• French energy giant Total [goes on](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/total-goes-on-mideast-oil-buying-spree-adding-to-market-turmoil) Middle East oil-buying spree, adding to market turmoil as companies scramble to secure alternative supplies, per Bloomberg.

• UAE real estate developers [are working](https://asharqbusiness.com/amp/economics/125554/%D9%85%D8%B7%D9%88%D8%B1%D9%88-%D8%B9%D9%82%D8%A7%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D9%81%D9%8A-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%A5%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D9%8A%D8%B3%D8%B9%D9%88%D9%86-%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%AD%D8%AA%D9%88%D8%A7%D8%A1-%D9%82%D9%84%D9%82-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D8%B3%D8%AA%D8%AB%D9%85%D8%B1%D9%8A%D9%86/) to contain investor concerns about the Iran war, according to Asharq Business.

**Country Watch**

**Saudi Arabia**
Prince Khalid bin Salman discussed Iranian attacks on the Kingdom with Hungary's defense minister, condemning Iran's "flagrant attacks" in a phone call. Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan is attending G7 meetings in France, participating in sessions on global governance reform and meeting with EU officials on regional developments.

**UAE** 
UAE air defenses [engaged 15](https://twitter.com/modgovae/status/1840329876543210987) ballistic missiles and 11 UAVs from Iran on March 26, according to the Defense Ministry. Since the start of Iranian attacks, UAE systems have intercepted 372 ballistic missiles and 15 UAVs total. The ministry confirmed that sounds heard across the country resulted from interception systems and fighter aircraft operations.

**Qatar**
Qatar's meteorological department warned of heavy rains and thunderstorms for March 26-27, with government agencies continuing digital services. Prime Minister Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani condemned Iranian attacks on Qatar as "rejected and unjustified," while advisor Majed Al Ansari met with EU Gulf representative Luigi Di Maio.

**Kuwait**
A fire broke out in a fuel tank at Kuwait Airport [following a](https://aawsat.com/%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B9%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B9%D8%B1%D8%A8%D9%8A/%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%AE%D9%84%D9%8A%D8%AC/5254983-%D8%AD%D8%B1%D9%8A%D9%82-%D9%81%D9%8A-%D8%AE%D8%B2%D8%A7%D9%86-%D9%88%D9%82%D9%88%D8%AF-%D8%A8%D9%85%D8%B7%D8%A7%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%83%D9%88%D9%8A%D8%AA-%D8%A8%D8%B9%D8%AF-%D9%87%D8%AC%D9%88%D9%85-%D8%A8%D9%85%D9%8F%D8%B3%D9%8A%D9%91%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%AA) drone attack, according to Asharq Al-Awsat. Lebanon's Foreign Ministry condemned the "terrorist plot" against Kuwait.

**Oman**
[Two Omani](https://timesofoman.com//article/169917-two-omani-women-drown-in-ibri-water-pool) women drowned in an Ibri water pool, Times of Oman reports. Foreign Minister Badr Albusaidi met with EU Gulf representative Luigi Di Maio, expressing Oman's commitment to regional peace and working on Strait of Hormuz safe passage arrangements.

**Yemen**
Saudi Yemen envoy Mohammed AlJabir announced Al-Hudaydah has been "liberated," posting that "Yemen is breathing." He also highlighted that nearly 2 million Yemenis work in Saudi Arabia, transferring over $4 billion annually to support 15 million people in Yemen. Houthi spokesman Mohammed Abdulsalam signed an agreement addressing banking restrictions and resuming Yemenia Airlines flights between Sanaa and Jordan.

**Official Signals**

Saudi Interior Ministry announced it will process visa holders whose permits expired as of February 25, 2026, who were unable to leave due to regional conditions. The ministry emphasized this follows "leadership directives." UAE Defense Ministry's detailed missile intercept counts signal systematic documentation of Iranian attacks, with officials emphasizing the "impossible" nature of targets versus "cheap" missiles. Qatar's Prime Minister Al Thani's direct condemnation of Iranian attacks as "unjustified" marks a clear policy position from Doha.
