**Gulf Brief**

1. **Zelenskyy Saudi visit.** Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy arrived in Saudi Arabia for "important meetings" with Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman, according to [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/ukraines-zelenskiy-arrives-saudi-arabia-important-meetings-2026-03-26/). The visit focuses on Ukraine providing anti-drone expertise to Gulf states facing Iranian attacks. Saudi Arabia's Crown Prince discussed bilateral relations and regional developments including Middle East military escalation during the Jeddah meeting.

2. **Iran war disrupts LNG.** War damage to Qatar has significantly impacted global liquefied natural gas outlook and upended Asian demand growth, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/iran-war-damage-qatar-hits-global-lng-outlook-upends-asia-demand-growth-2026-03-26/). The disruption affects Qatar's position as a major LNG supplier to Asian markets. Energy infrastructure targeting raises risks of long-term supply chain disruption across the Gulf region.

3. **Iran formalizes Hormuz tolls.** Iran has begun implementing a "toll booth" regime for ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz, [AP reports](https://apnews.com/article/iran-hormuz-shipping-tolls-china-de5159966cde7de7b964b3c2c67eec07). The move formalizes Tehran's chokehold on the critical waterway through which much of global oil supplies pass. The development escalates tensions over freedom of navigation in Gulf waters.

4. **Saudi intercepts drones.** Saudi air defenses intercepted and destroyed three drones in the eastern region, according to the Saudi Defense Ministry's [official statement](https://www.youm7.com/story/2026/3/27/%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%AF%D9%81%D8%A7%D8%B9-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B3%D8%B9%D9%88%D8%AF%D9%8A%D8%A9-%D8%A7%D8%B9%D8%AA%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%B6-%D9%88%D8%AA%D8%AF%D9%85%D9%8A%D8%B1-3-%D9%85%D8%B3%D9%8A%D9%91%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D9%81%D9%8A-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D9%86%D8%B7%D9%82%D8%A9-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B4%D1%8A%D1%82%D9%82%D9%8A%D8%A9/7354935). The interceptions occurred as Iranian attacks continue against Saudi territory. Defense Minister Prince Khalid bin Salman discussed Iran's "flagrant attacks" on the Kingdom in calls with Hungarian and French counterparts.

5. **UAE air defenses engage missiles.** UAE air defenses intercepted 15 ballistic missiles and 11 drones launched from Iran on March 26, the Defense Ministry reported on Twitter. Since Iranian attacks began, UAE systems have engaged 372 ballistic missiles and over 2,000 projectiles total. The ministry confirmed sounds across the country resulted from interception systems and fighter aircraft operations.

6. **Trump extends Iran pause.** President Trump extended his pause on striking Iran's energy facilities into April, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/trump-pauses-attacks-irans-energy-plants-says-talks-are-going-well-2026-03-26/). The move comes as diplomatic talks continue between Washington and Tehran. Iran's Foreign Minister separately stated there are no current negotiations with the United States, reflecting ongoing diplomatic tensions.

7. **Saudi oil sales drop.** Saudi oil sales to top Asian buyers will decline due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions, [Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/saudi-oil-sales-to-top-asia-buyers-to-drop-on-hormuz-disruptions). The shipping constraints affect Riyadh's key export markets across Asia. Total meanwhile goes on a Middle East oil buying spree, adding to market volatility during the regional conflict.

8. **Qatar PM discusses war.** Qatari Prime Minister Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani met with US Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth in Washington to discuss regional developments. Qatar's official Twitter account confirmed Iranian attacks on the state "especially deliberately targeting capabilities of the Qatari people" are rejected under any pretext. The Prime Minister stressed Qatar's trust in its leadership and armed forces.

9. **Oman road closure.** Mountain Road in Muscat was closed as a precautionary measure, [Times of Oman reports](https://timesofoman.com//article/169922-mountain-road-in-muscat-closed-as-a-precautionary-measure). The closure relates to ongoing weather conditions affecting the sultanate. Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Albusaidi met with EU Gulf envoy Luigi Di Maio to discuss regional peace efforts.

10. **Saudi visa extension.** Saudi Arabia's Interior Ministry announced processing arrangements for visa holders whose permits expired February 25, 2026, who cannot leave due to current regional conditions. The ministry's [Twitter account](https://twitter.com/MOISaudiArabia) detailed procedures for visit, Umrah, transit and exit visa holders affected by the situation. The move addresses travelers stranded by ongoing regional tensions.
