**Gulf States Daily Brief**

1. **Trump delays Iran strikes.** US President Trump [postponed planned](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/3/27/iran-war-live-trump-delays-attacks-on-iranian-energy-sector-by-10-days) attacks on Iranian energy infrastructure by 10 days, Al Jazeera reports. The delay comes as Iran continues missile and drone strikes against Gulf states, with UAE air defenses intercepting 15 ballistic missiles and 11 drones on March 26.

2. **Ukrainian president visits Saudi Arabia.** President Zelensky arrived in Riyadh for meetings with Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman, [according to Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/ukraines-zelenskiy-arrives-saudi-arabia-important-meetings-2026-03-26/). The visit focuses on Ukraine's drone expertise against Iranian weapons systems as part of a broader Gulf states tour.

3. **Qatar LNG exports disrupted.** Iran war damage to Qatar has upended global LNG outlook and Asian demand growth, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/iran-war-damage-qatar-hits-global-lng-outlook-upends-asia-demand-growth-2026-03-26/). Qatar Airways is seeking cost cuts as Iranian attacks halt flights across the region.

4. **Saudi oil shipments to Asia fall.** Saudi Arabia's oil sales to top Asian buyers will drop due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions, [Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/saudi-oil-sales-to-top-asia-buyers-to-drop-on-hormuz-disruptions). French energy giant Total is buying Middle Eastern oil supplies following regional supply chain disruptions.

5. **UAE intercepts Iranian missiles.** UAE air defenses engaged 372 ballistic missiles and over 2,100 drones since Iranian attacks began, the Defense Ministry announced. Since the conflict started, UAE systems have intercepted hundreds of projectiles targeting the Emirates.

6. **Iranian sports teams banned from travel.** Iran prohibited its sports teams from traveling to countries it deems hostile, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/sports/iran-bans-sports-teams-travelling-countries-it-deems-hostile-2026-03-26/). The ban reflects escalating regional tensions following ongoing military exchanges.

7. **Maersk maintains Gulf land routes.** Shipping giant Maersk says Gulf land-bridge routes retain capacity for food and medicines despite regional conflicts, [according to Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/maersk-says-gulf-land-bridge-routes-still-have-capacity-food-medicines-2026-03-26/). Alternative shipping corridors remain operational for essential supplies.

8. **Saudi Interior extends visa deadlines.** The Saudi Interior Ministry will process expired visas for visitors unable to depart due to regional conditions. The extension applies to all visit, Umrah, transit and exit visas expiring as of February 25, 2026.

9. **Two Omani women drown.** Two Omani women died in an Ibri water pool incident, [Times of Oman reports](https://timesofoman.com//article/169917-two-omani-women-drown-in-ibri-water-pool). Authorities are investigating the drowning in the northern governorate location.

10. **Yemen prisoner exchange talks.** Houthi negotiator Mohammed Abdulsalam announced agreements to cancel banking restrictions and resume Yemenia Airlines flights between Sanaa and Jordan. The deals include daily flights to Cairo and India as part of broader prisoner exchange discussions.
