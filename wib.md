**Gulf War Update: March 29, 2026**

1. **Iranian escalation continues.** Iran launched [16 ballistic missiles](https://www.twitter.com/modgovae) and 42 drones at UAE targets on March 29, according to the UAE Defense Ministry, as the monthlong conflict intensified. Houthi forces from Yemen joined the war with attacks on Israel, while more US troops arrived in the region to support Gulf allies.

2. **US suffers first major losses.** Bloomberg News [reports Iran](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-29/us-sees-first-combat-loss-of-valuable-e-3-jet-in-missile-strike) destroyed a US E-3 AWACS aircraft and hit air tankers in recent strikes, marking the first combat loss of the valuable surveillance jets. US troops were also injured in an Iranian attack on a Saudi base housing American forces.

3. **Saudi pipeline bypasses Hormuz crisis.** Saudi Arabia is pumping 7 million barrels per day through its East-West pipeline, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/saudi-pipeline-pumping-7-million-bpd-oil-bypassing-hormuz-bloomberg-news-reports-2026-03-28/), allowing the kingdom to export oil while avoiding the increasingly dangerous Strait of Hormuz. The pipeline provides critical energy security as regional tensions threaten shipping routes.

4. **Gulf states impose security measures.** Bahrain banned maritime movement from 6 PM to 4 AM starting March 29, while Kuwait shot down four Iranian drones, according to Arabic media reports. The UAE has intercepted 414 ballistic missiles and 15 cruise missiles since Iran's attacks began, defense officials said.

5. **Diplomatic push for peace.** Pakistan hosted talks with Saudi Arabia, Turkey and Egypt as regional powers seek to [contain the crisis](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/pakistan-host-talks-with-saudi-arabia-turkey-egypt-amid-iran-war-diplomacy-2026-03-28/), Reuters reports. Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan participated in the Islamabad meeting, emphasizing the need for diplomatic solutions to prevent further escalation.

6. **Industrial targets hit.** Iranian strikes damaged aluminum production facilities in Bahrain and the UAE, with two people injured at Bahrain's aluminum company, according to Arabic media reports. The attacks marked an escalation to civilian infrastructure beyond military targets.

7. **UN forms Hormuz task force.** The UN Secretary-General established a special task force to address the humanitarian crisis in the Strait of Hormuz, the Times of Oman [reports](https://timesofoman.com//article/170020-un-secretary-general-forms-special-task-force-to-address-humanitarian-crisis-in-strait-of-hormuz), as shipping disruptions threaten global supply chains and regional food security.

8. **Zelenskyy seeks Gulf defense ties.** Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy visited UAE and Qatar to discuss defense cooperation and secure long-term fuel supply deals, [AP reports](https://apnews.com/article/russia-ukraine-war-odesa-drones-zelenskyy-gulf-5d520d03324170efbfb7f75ca6f2492e). The timing coincided with Gulf states seeking to diversify their security partnerships beyond traditional allies.

9. **Markets decline on war fears.** Most Gulf stock markets fell on concerns about broader regional conflict, [Reuters reports](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/most-gulf-markets-ease-fears-broader-iran-conflict-2026-03-29/), while memory chip shares globally shed $100 billion as AI hardware shortages eased. Energy prices remained volatile on supply disruption concerns.

10. **Iran's parliament defiant.** Iranian Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf rejected what he called a US "15-Point Plan," telling the Times of Oman that ["We will never be humiliated"](https://timesofoman.com//article/170022-we-will-never-be-humiliated-iran-speaker-ghalibaf-slams-us-15-point-plan) in response to American diplomatic overtures. Tehran warned against any US ground invasion as regional diplomats sought an end to hostilities.
