1. **Ukrainian defense deals.** Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy signed defense cooperation agreements with Saudi Arabia during a surprise Gulf tour, offering air defense technology in exchange for funding. Bloomberg [reported the](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-27/zelenskiy-offers-air-defense-for-gulf-funds-in-saudi-arabia-deal) deal aims to counter Iranian drone threats. Zelenskyy is visiting multiple Gulf states as Ukraine seeks new military partnerships following escalating regional tensions.

2. **Hormuz shipping crisis.** Chinese vessels halted attempts to exit the Strait of Hormuz despite Iranian assurances of safe passage, according to Reuters [reporting on](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/chinese-ships-halt-attempt-exit-hormuz-despite-iran-safe-passage-assurances-2026-03-27/) the shipping disruption. UAE ports increased operations to handle oil flows bypassing the strategic waterway. The closure threatens global supply chains for food, medicines, and electronics, the BBC noted.

3. **Iranian sports ban.** Iran prohibited its national sports teams from traveling to "hostile" countries, AP [reported as](https://apnews.com/article/iran-saudi-arabia-war-soccer-58a6347b3bab91b18b5cdd444a2a417d) regional tensions affect athletic competition. The decision follows escalating military confrontations between Iran and Gulf states. Iranian attacks on Saudi Arabia and other regional countries have disrupted normal diplomatic and cultural exchanges.

4. **UAE air defenses activated.** UAE air defense systems intercepted six ballistic missiles and nine drones launched from Iran on March 27, the Defense Ministry confirmed on Twitter. Since Iranian attacks began, UAE forces have [engaged 378](https://twitter.com/modgovae) ballistic missiles total. The ministry emphasized ongoing threats to UAE territory from Iranian military operations.

5. **Oil market volatility.** Oil prices dropped after Trump delayed timeline for strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure, Bloomberg [reported as](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-27) markets reacted to diplomatic signals. Regional war has distorted vital oil price benchmarks. Trump claimed advances in ceasefire negotiations while Israel warned attacks on Iran would "escalate and expand."

6. **Oman weather emergency.** Heavy rainfall caused road damage and power outages across North Al Sharqiyah governorate, with health centers [suspending services](https://timesofoman.com//article/169942-temporary-suspension-of-services-at-several-health-centres-due-to-weather-conditions) due to weather conditions. Two citizens were arrested in Bidiyah for risky wadi crossing during flooding. Mobile connectivity alerts were issued for Al Dhahirah and Musandam regions.

7. **Saudi G7 participation.** Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan participated in G7 ministerial meetings, discussing transnational threats and regional security. His Twitter account detailed meetings with India's external affairs minister on [bilateral relations](https://twitter.com/FaisalbinFarhan) and regional developments. The sessions covered navigation security, cross-border crime, and mineral supply chains during ongoing regional instability.

8. **Palestinian state conference.** Saudi Arabia and France co-chaired an international conference on Palestinian statehood implementation and the two-state solution. Prince Faisal bin Farhan praised France's determination to recognize Palestine, stating it [strengthens the](https://twitter.com/FaisalbinFarhan) path to lasting peace. The kingdom reaffirmed its unwavering position on establishing an independent Palestinian state.

9. **Global fertilizer shortage.** The Iran war sparked worldwide fertilizer shortages threatening food prices, as AP [reported the](https://apnews.com/article/iran-war-fertilizer-exports-farming-3b7c92d58dba0817c3aa8f1db47464b7) agricultural impact of regional conflict. Iran traditionally exports significant fertilizer volumes globally. Emerging economies' debt financing froze as war developments disrupted international markets.

10. **Pakistan mediation role.** Pakistan emerged as a mediator between the US and Iran, leveraging its historical diplomatic channels from Nixon to Trump administrations. AP [examined Pakistan's](https://apnews.com/article/pakistan-us-iran-war-emerging-peace-mediator-f4e809dd3f93b3d67b54f9d75d33d55c) unique position as both countries seek diplomatic solutions. The nation previously facilitated backchannel communications between rival powers during Cold War tensions.
