Gulf Brief — March 28, 2026

1. Iranian strike on Saudi base. Iran struck Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, wounding at least 10 US service members and damaging several aircraft, according to AP. The attack represents a significant escalation in Iran's targeting of American forces stationed in Gulf states.

2. Gulf states demand Iran capabilities degraded. Gulf countries told the US that ending the current conflict is insufficient and Iran's military capabilities must be degraded, Reuters reports. The position reflects growing Gulf frustration with Washington's handling of Iranian threats to regional security.

3. Ukraine signs Saudi defense deal. Ukraine and Saudi Arabia signed a defense cooperation agreement, with President Zelenskyy offering air defense systems in exchange for Gulf funding, per Bloomberg. The deal aims to counter Iranian drone capabilities affecting both regions.

4. Chinese ships halt Hormuz transit. Chinese vessels stopped attempting to exit the Strait of Hormuz despite Iranian assurances of safe passage, Reuters reports. The development highlights shipping concerns over the vital energy transit route.

5. G7 emphasizes Hormuz security. G7 Foreign Ministers stressed the "absolute necessity" of keeping the Strait of Hormuz "safe and toll-free," according to the Times of Oman. Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Faisal participated in the G7 ministerial meeting.

6. Yemen launches first missile at Israel. Houthi forces fired their first missile at Israel since the current conflict began, AP reports. The strike marks a potential expansion of Ansar Allah's targeting beyond Red Sea shipping operations.

7. UAE air defenses engage Iranian attacks. UAE air defenses intercepted six ballistic missiles and nine drones launched from Iran on March 27, according to the Defense Ministry. Since Iranian attacks began, UAE systems have engaged 378 ballistic missiles, the ministry confirmed on Twitter.

8. Trump pressures Saudi Israel recognition. President Trump pressed Saudi Arabia to recognize Israel as he defended the Iran conflict, Bloomberg reports. The push comes as the administration seeks broader regional normalization following military operations.

9. Russia bans gasoline exports. Russia announced a ban on gasoline exports beginning April 1, according to the Times of Oman. The restriction could affect global fuel markets already strained by Middle East tensions.

10. Iran forbids sports travel. Iran banned its sports teams from traveling to "hostile" countries, AP reports. The restrictions reflect Tehran's broader isolation as the conflict continues.
