**Gulf Briefing - March 26, 2026**

Ukraine President Volodymyr Zelenskiy has arrived in Saudi Arabia for what he called "[important meetings](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/ukraines-zelenskiy-arrives-saudi-arabia-important-meetings-2026-03-26/)" as he launches a surprise Gulf states tour to pitch Ukraine's drone expertise, according to Reuters reports. The visit comes as Ukraine seeks to leverage its battlefield experience against Iranian drones to strengthen Gulf partnerships while the region faces [intensifying Iranian](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/newsfeed/2026/3/26/iranian-missiles-cause-widespread-damage-across-israel-in-latest-wave?traffic_source=rss) attacks. The BBC reports Zelenskiy is [offering Ukraine's](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2r4wxdw3no?at_medium=RSS&amp;at_campaign=rss) counter-drone capabilities to Gulf states dealing with Iranian missile and drone strikes.

The ongoing Iran conflict continues disrupting regional energy flows, with Bloomberg reporting that [Saudi oil sales](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/saudi-oil-sales-to-top-asia-buyers-to-drop-on-hormuz-disruptions) to major Asian buyers will drop due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions. Meanwhile, Qatar's LNG operations face [significant damage](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/iran-war-damage-qatar-hits-global-lng-outlook-upends-asia-demand-growth-2026-03-26/) from the Iran war, upending Asian demand growth, Reuters reports. Oil prices have [risen 5%](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-oil-prices-rise-investors-assess-middle-east-de-escalation-2026-03-25/) as investors fear further Middle East escalation.
